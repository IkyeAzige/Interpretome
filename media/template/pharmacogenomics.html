Genetics of Drug Response

Like many traits, clinical and otherwise, response to various drugs often has a significant genetic component. Traditional GWAS methods can be used to discover these variants, searching for associations between genotypes and efficacy of drugs.

In this exercise, you can explore variants that may alter your response to prescription drugs. These annotations are courtesy of Michelle Carillo and the PharmGKB team. The description provided is specific to your genotype at each locus. The "evidence level" is a measure of confidence in the annotation, based on evidence from the literature (and corresponds to the evidence level at PharmGKB). Additional annotations can be found at PharmGKB.

Note that these annotations are not intended as diagnoses. They are based on published scientific evidence, but have not been evaluated by the FDA for clinical decision making. While variants may alter your probability for a reaction to a drug (intended or adverse event), no single variant or combination of variants can definitively predict your pharmacogenomic response. The disclaimers from PharmGKB apply to the information on this website.
