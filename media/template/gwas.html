GWAS

Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) are commonly used methods to identify genetic factors associated with diseases. In a case/control GWAS, each SNP is stratified by genotype (2 alleles or a dominant/recessive genotype model) and phenotype (e.g. binary, such as "Wet" or "Dry" in the case of earwax, or a continuous variable such as height). The association between genotype and phenotype is then tested by a chi-squared test.

In this exercise, we will attempt to reproduce results from previous GWAS by identifying SNPs associated with earwax type, eye color, asparagus metabolite detection, bitter taste, and lactose intolerance.
