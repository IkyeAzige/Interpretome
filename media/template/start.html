    How are my data kept private?

    Your genome will not be sent to any server, it remains on your computer. This website will make requests to a database that only contain "rsid" (without genotypes) and "population" (self-reported in the top-right) information. At no point will any genotypes be sent across the wires (all computation will be done in the browser). Some exercises may have an option to submit personal information, including genotypes or results of analyses, which will be anonymously stored on a secure server.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can I receive a health report?

    No, there is little to no health interpretation on Interpretome, and we intentionally do not provide a comprehensive analysis like 23andMe did. You are free to explore the website, but note that the site is intended for educational and research purposes only. See disclaimer above.

    How do I use the site? How much does it cost?

    The site is free and is meant to be an interactive exploration of your genetic data. Click the "Begin Exploring!" button in the top right and load your genome data. Note that no data gets sent to our servers, which means you can be assured of privacy, but also means you must load your data every time you use the site.

    I'm considering using the site, but want to know what kind of information I might receive before I get or load my data.

    You can download sample genotype files (anonymous patients) here. Download one of the *.txt files, and then return to the page, click "Begin Exploring!", load the txt file in, and then explore the tabs of the site.

    Do you support FTDNA data?

    No, unfortunately because of the different set of SNPs they test, FTDNA data are not supported. While one could attempt to convert the data manually, this is not recommended as the results will be unstable.
