Example annotation file


Custom annotation files must be tab delimited, have a header row, and the first column must be the SNP rsid. For example, 


   # This line is ignored
   # Actually, any line that starts with a hash...
   rsid        Disease/Trait   count(Risk Alelle)   Comments   
   rs3094315   RLS             A             This is a SNP
   rs3934834   Earwax          G             G = wet earwax
   rs9442372   High IQ         C             Not an association
			      

			        Surrounding a column header with count(*) as with the "Risk Allele" column in the example above
			        indicates that the user's genotype will be compared against the column and a summary
			        of total counts will be displayed with the results.

Exploration

Part of the power of genome interpretation is that the wealth of knowledge spread across the biomedical literature can be applied to a personal genome. While not all annotations will be applicable to every individual in every condition, and reverse inferences (applying large studies to an individual) are not always valid, there is still much to be learned in exploring the biological and clinical effect of variants.

This section contains exercises for the lectures of the Stanford course in Personalized Medicine and Genomics.

Explore the exercises available or load your own: the world is your oyster.
